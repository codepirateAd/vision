cv2.split

The cv2.split() method splits the image into three channels - b,g,r channels

import cv2
img =cv2.imread('tomatoes.jpg')

b,g,r = cv2.split(img)

cv2.imshow("Blue",b)
cv2.imshow("Green",g)
cv2.imshow("Red",r)

cv2.waitKey()
cv2.destroyAllWindows()


















cv2.merge

The cv2.merge() method merges the image from three channels - b,g,r into a single image 

 1
 2
 3
 4
 5
 6
 7
 8

import cv2
img =cv2.imread('tomatoes.jpg')

b,g,r = cv2.split(img)
merged = cv2.merge((b,g,r))

cv2.imshow("Merged",merged)

cv2.waitKey()
cv2.destroyAllWindows()




ROI (Region of Interest)

In ROI we manipulate or capture certain parts of the image and reuse it.

import cv2
img =cv2.imread('tomatoes.jpg')

part_of_img = img[120:200,150:200]
img[20:100,0:50] = part_of_img

cv2.imshow("ROI",img)

cv2.waitKey()
cv2.destroyAllWindows()




cv2.add

We can add two images with the OpenCV function, cv.add(). Both images should be of same depth and type, or the second image can just be a scalar value.

import cv2
img =cv2.imread('tomatoes.jpg')
frame = cv2.imread('frame.png')

dst = cv2.add(frame,img)

cv2.imshow("Image",dst)

cv2.waitKey()
cv2.destroyAllWindows()
